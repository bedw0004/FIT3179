Consumption-based CO₂ emissions per capita vs. GDP per capita

To bring emissions down towards net-zero is arguably the world’s biggest challenge in the short-term future.

But the world’s energy problem is actually even larger than that, because the world has not one, but two energy problems. 1. Developing countries with low carbon emissions lack access to energy. This is seen in the left-hand side of the scatter plot.

2. Richer countries produce excessive greenhouse gas emissions. This is seen in the right-hand side of the scatter plot.

How does each country stack up?

The line charts above show the downward trend of CO2 emissions per capita in the countries with higher GDP per capita in recent years.

While in countries with lower GDP per capita, the emissions curve is flattening, but is not decreasing. Richer countries need to help developing nations to transition their energy habits if the world is to have any hope of meeting its emissions targets.

The obligation is on the more fortunate of us, to help those who aren't so lucky.
